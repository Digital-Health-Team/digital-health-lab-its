# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Students (Creators/Submitters):** Undergraduate and postgraduate students at ITS who submit their innovations — 3D models, open-source applications, academic work — to be archived and made discoverable. They use the platform to gain visibility for their work and to order custom fabrication services. Context: typically on campus, motivated by academic deadlines and the pride of publishing something real.

**Public Users (Browsers and Clients):** Visitors browsing the innovation archive or placing made-to-order requests (3D printing, custom design). They arrive with curiosity or a specific fabrication need, and need to quickly assess whether IDIG can solve their problem. The platform must project enough authority and clarity to convert a cautious visitor into a paying client.

## Product Purpose

IDIG Health Tech is the digital face of the Medical Technology Laboratory at Institut Teknologi Sepuluh Nopember (ITS), Indonesia. It serves two intertwined functions: a public-facing repository that archives and showcases student innovations (3D models, open-source software, academic publications), and a made-to-order service platform for 3D printing and custom design work.

Success looks like: a student's prototype gaining real-world visibility; a client ordering a print with confidence; and the lab's expertise speaking for itself without explanation.

## Brand Personality

Authoritative, precise, pioneering.

The brand is a professional and innovative doctor: someone with deep domain mastery who communicates clearly and confidently, without jargon or showmanship. Not cold or clinical — there is warmth in the mission of sharing knowledge — but always grounded in rigor.

Voice: measured, expert, bilingual (Indonesian primary, English secondary). Never chatty, never hustle-culture. The platform speaks like a senior researcher, not a startup.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-generated websites: icon grids, rainbow-gradient blobs, "innovation" stock imagery, identical rounded cards repeated endlessly.
- Sterile hospital whites with teal accents — clinical minimalism that feels antiseptic rather than medical-professional.
- Generic university portal design: crowded information architecture, committee-by-committee navigation, dated typography.
- Vercel / Linear / Stripe aesthetic — dark-mode SaaS minimalism. The platform is institution-rooted, not tech-startup.

## Design Principles

1. **Authority before novelty.** Every visual choice — the deep navy, the ECG line, the hexagon pattern — earns its place as a domain-metaphor, not decoration. If a visual element cannot be explained by the domain (medical technology, materials science, academic rigor), remove it.

2. **The innovations are the hero.** Student work is the content the platform exists to showcase. Layout decisions should direct the eye toward the archive and the capabilities demonstrated there, not toward platform chrome or marketing claims.

3. **Depth signals precision.** Layered dark backgrounds, controlled glow, and translucent surfaces communicate the complexity of the underlying work. Visual depth is earned by what the lab actually does, not applied for style.

4. **Bilingual by design.** All UI copy and structural decisions must serve both Indonesian-speaking students and international visitors with equal clarity. Language is not an afterthought.

5. **Motion reinforces meaning.** The heartbeat animation is purposeful because the domain is health technology. Every animated element must justify itself by what it communicates, not how it looks.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. No dark mode toggle (dark-first design confirmed). Reduced motion preference must suppress ECG and pulse animations. Color contrast checked against both dark navy and teal backgrounds. No reliance on color alone for state communication (badges and status indicators must carry text labels alongside color).
